# Tracklete Website Rebuild Brief

Backup date: 2026-06-02

Use this document when starting over with the Tracklete website. It explains what the website is about, who it serves, what application features need to be communicated, and what content should appear. For the full raw copy inventory, see `WEBSITE_CONTENT_BACKUP.md`.

## One-Sentence Summary

Tracklete is a performance and operations platform for rowing athletes, coaches, clubs, and federations, combining training schedules, attendance, athlete readiness, fitness analytics, bodystats, integrations, and club-wide oversight in one product.

## Core Positioning

Tracklete should be presented as one clear command center for rowing programmes.

The main promise:

One performance platform for every seat in the boat.

The website should make clear that Tracklete replaces scattered tools, spreadsheets, chat messages, manual attendance tracking, and disconnected training data with one calm, structured view for athletes, coaches, and clubs.

## Primary Audiences

### Athletes

Athletes use Tracklete to:

- See their training schedule.
- Log attendance and availability.
- Track their own training data.
- Complete daily bodystats and wellness check-ins.
- Monitor progress over time.
- Sync training data from external platforms.

Messaging angle:

Track your training, own your progress.

### Coaches

Coaches use Tracklete to:

- Plan sessions and weekly training blocks.
- See attendance across the squad.
- Review readiness, sleep, HRV, resting heart rate, and injury notes.
- Compare training load, form, and fitness signals.
- Review erg scores, GPS data, and weekly analytics.
- Spot issues before they become missed sessions or poor race preparation.

Messaging angle:

See the whole crew in one view.

### Clubs And Federations

Clubs and federations use Tracklete to:

- Manage multiple teams.
- Standardize training and attendance workflows.
- Compare crews and athlete groups.
- Support club-wide analysis.
- Coordinate coaches, athletes, and staff.
- Scale from one team to an entire programme.

Messaging angle:

Run your entire programme from one platform.

## Product Features To Explain

The redesign should clearly communicate these features.

### Training Schedules

Tracklete includes training planning for rowing, erg, bike, recovery, and other sessions. A session can include time, sport, description, notes, attached context, and attendance responses.

Example sessions:

- 06:30 Row: Starts + race pieces
- 18:00 Erg: 3x15 min threshold
- 07:15 Bike: Recovery spin + mobility

### Attendance And Availability

Attendance is built directly into the schedule. Coaches can see who is going, maybe going, or has not responded. This avoids using a separate attendance tool.

Important message:

Every session shows who responded, who is going, and coach notes.

### Bodystats And Wellness

Athletes can complete lightweight daily check-ins. The site should mention:

- Body weight
- Resting heart rate
- HRV
- Sleep
- Feeling / rate
- Injury notes
- Comments

Important message:

Daily check-ins should be lightweight enough to complete every morning, but useful enough for coaches to act on.

### Fitness Intelligence

Tracklete includes a dedicated fitness view built from GPS heart-rate data. The website should mention:

- CTL fitness
- ATL fatigue
- Form / TSB
- ACWR
- Fitness and fatigue trends
- Race-readiness cues
- Short-term forecast / taper signal

Important message:

Coaches get more than weekly totals without needing a separate analysis tool.

### Weekly Analytics

The product summarizes training activity across a week.

Example metrics:

- Total distance
- Time by activity
- Heart rate zones
- Weekly change versus last week
- Crew load

Example values used in current content:

- 84.2 km total distance
- +12.4 km vs last week
- Row 220 min
- Erg 75 min
- Bike 45 min

### Ergometer And Concept2 Tracking

Tracklete supports ergometer score tracking and Concept2 workflows. The website should mention:

- Ergometer tracking
- Concept2 Logbook synchronization
- Erg pieces linked to events
- Score comparison across athletes

Example pieces:

- 2k test
- 6x500m
- 4x10 min
- 30R20

### Strava And External Imports

Tracklete syncs activities from external systems, especially Strava and Concept2.

Important message:

Activities import without manual entry, so the weekly picture is always current.

### Club Analysis And Leaderboards

Tracklete should be shown as useful beyond a single athlete or coach. Club-level views should include:

- Athlete comparison
- Crew comparison
- Distance, time, power, and erg score analysis
- Leaderboards
- Club-wide reporting

Example groups:

- Women VIII
- Development crew
- Lightweights

### Coach Overview

The website should include a dashboard-style proof point showing how a coach can scan the squad before a session.

Important dashboard signals:

- Who is going tomorrow
- Who logged bodystats
- Crew load
- Sleep
- Resting heart rate
- HRV
- Readiness flags

## Messaging Principles

Use direct, practical product language.

Prefer:

- "See the whole crew in one view."
- "Attendance is built into every session."
- "Daily check-ins coaches can act on."
- "Strava and Concept2 keep data in sync."
- "Compare athletes and crews across any metric."

Avoid:

- Generic sports-tech hype.
- Claims that sound like medical advice.
- Overpromising performance improvement.
- Making Tracklete sound like a general fitness app.
- Making the site too abstract or too corporate.

## Tone Of Voice

The tone should be:

- Clear
- Calm
- Confident
- Operational
- Rowing-specific
- Coach-friendly
- Practical rather than flashy

Tracklete should feel like a serious tool built by people who understand rowing programmes.

## Content Priorities For A Rebuild

If rebuilding from scratch, preserve these in order:

1. Product positioning and audience explanation.
